Chapter 5

Just Because

"Some love doesn’t announce itself. It just quietly shows up."

The next morning felt like any other. The alarm rang too early. The train was crowded. Work started before coffee. Around noon, my phone lit up. Him: Lunch? Me: Trying to. My boss has other plans. Him: Good luck then. Don’t forget to eat. By evening, we met at the station as usual. "You look tired," he said. "I am." "You'll survive." "I hope so." We laughed. Then he handed me a small paper bag. "For you." Inside were warm pazham pori. "You actually got this?" "My mom made them." "You asked her?" "You said it yesterday." A smile came before I could stop it. "You remembered?" "You say a lot of things. I remember some of them." It wasn’t a big gift. But it felt like one. The walk continued like always. Work. Train. Random thoughts. Nothing special. But somehow everything felt softer. At the station, we parted ways. "See you tomorrow?" he asked. "If my boss allows it," I laughed. "I'll pray for you." "You better." And we walked away. Different directions. Same thought lingering in both minds. Sometimes affection doesn’t need a reason. It just shows up. Just because.
