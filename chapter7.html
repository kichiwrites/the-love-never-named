Chapter 7

The Prayer We Carried

The last note of the worship song faded as everyone settled into their seats.

"Before we end today's youth meeting," the youth leader smiled, holding up a wooden bowl, "we're going to do something a little different."

Curious murmurs filled the room. He placed a stack of small white papers on the table.

"I want each of you to write down one prayer request. No names. No explanations. Just one thing you want someone to pray for this week. We'll each pick one randomly and pray for that person until next Sunday."

The room grew quiet.

Pens clicked open.

Heads lowered. Some people started writing immediately. Others stared at the blank paper, thinking.

Grace uncapped her pen.

She knew exactly what she wanted to write.

Her fingers hovered over the paper.

For a brief moment, her eyes became distant.

She wrote three words.

Then stopped.

She looked at them.

Quickly, she folded the paper before anyone could accidentally glance at it.

A few seconds later, she unfolded it again.

Slowly...

She tore it into tiny pieces.

No one noticed.

Except Noel.

He didn't stare.

He simply looked away.

Grace picked up another slip of paper.

This time she wrote something simple.

"Pray that I continue to trust God's timing."

She folded it neatly and dropped it into the bowl.

Across the room, Noel quietly folded his own prayer.

"Pray that I never fail the people God has entrusted to me."

After everyone had placed their prayers into the bowl, it was passed around once more.

"Take one," the leader said.

"But don't try to guess who wrote it."

Grace unfolded hers.

"Pray that I never fail the people God has entrusted to me."

A small smile appeared on her face.

Whoever wrote this... loves people deeply.

She carefully tucked the paper inside her Bible.

Noel unfolded his.

"Pray that I continue to trust God's timing."

He smiled to himself.

"I can do that," he whispered.

For the next seven days...

Neither of them missed a prayer.

Every night before sleeping, Grace opened her Bible, read the same sentence, and prayed.

"Lord... Help this person love well without carrying burdens they were never meant to carry alone."

Across the city...

Noel prayed too.

"Father... Strengthen whoever wrote this. Help them trust You... Even when waiting feels difficult."

Neither knew...

They were praying for each other.

The following Sunday...

The youth gathered again.

"So," the leader grinned, "did everyone remember to pray?"

Hands went up across the room.

"Good."

"Now go find the person whose prayer you carried."

The hall instantly filled with laughter.

People compared handwriting.

Some guessed wrong.

Some hugged.

Grace still stood holding her folded paper.

Then she heard a familiar voice.

"Grace."

She turned.

"Monkey."

He scratched the back of his neck.

"I think... you have mine."

She unfolded the paper again.

"Pray that I never fail the people God has entrusted to me."

"You wrote this?"

He nodded.

"I've been praying for you every night."

"Thank you."

His smile softened.

"Whoever got mine... I hope they actually prayed."

Grace gently hit his arm.

"I did!"

"I know."

"I meant before I knew it was you."

He laughed.

"I know."

People slowly began leaving.

The church compound became quieter.

Grace and Noel walked toward the bus stop together.

She was unusually silent.

Noel noticed.

"You didn't write what you wanted to."

She stopped walking.

"...How do you know?"

"You folded the first paper before you wrote the second one."

She looked down.

"You notice everything."

"Only the important things."

For a few moments...

Only the sound of passing traffic filled the silence.

Then Grace spoke.

"There are some prayers... that only God and one other person know."

Noel understood immediately.

He didn't ask what she had wanted to write.

He didn't need to.

Instead, he nodded.

"And that's okay."

"I know everyone here would've been kind."

"I know nobody would've judged me."

"But..."

"I couldn't put that part of my heart into a bowl and let a stranger carry it."

Noel's voice was gentle.

"You don't have to."

Another silence.

Then Grace smiled faintly.

"That prayer already has enough people praying for it."

"God..."

"...and you."

For the first time since they had left church...

Noel smiled too.

"You never have to write it down."

"I'll keep praying anyway."

Grace blinked back the tears that threatened to escape.

She nudged his shoulder.

"Thank you, Monkey."

He nudged her back.

"Always."

They continued walking.

Talking about completely unrelated things.

What to eat.

Who forgot the church keys.

Why Aaron was always late.

As if the conversation they had just shared had never happened.

But somehow...

It stayed with both of them.

Long after they had gone home.

"Some prayers are shared with a congregation. Others are whispered only to God... and to the friend He knew you would need." 🤍
