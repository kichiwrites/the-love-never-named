Chapter 4

That One Message

"Sometimes a single message can stay longer than an entire day."

Some days are so busy that they leave no room for conversations, only thoughts. That morning, I rushed out of the house with barely enough time to catch my train. Between getting ready, packing my bag, and trying not to be late for work, I forgot to text him like I usually did. The day only got busier…. Meetings…Calls… A never ending list of tasks. And to top it all off, there was an office party in the evening. Meanwhile… Miles away, he went about his day too. He noticed there wasn't the usual "Good morning." No random complaint about the train. No photo of a cloudy sky. Nothing. He thought about texting first. More than once. But he waited. Hours passed. Then finally… His screen lit up. "It's raining... Feels like the perfect time for pazham pori, coffee... and you." He smiled. "Me?" "Yeah. The combo is incomplete without my best friend. 😉" Best friend. That word stayed with him. Later… I called him. For the next hour, I complained about everything. He just listened. And somehow, that was enough. By the end of the call, nothing had changed in my life. But something in me felt lighter. Across the city, he set his phone down too. Neither of them knew what this was becoming. Only that talking to each other made everything feel better. That night, he asked his mother, "Amma… can you make pazham pori tomorrow?" Not because he craved it. But because a message had stayed longer than the rain.
