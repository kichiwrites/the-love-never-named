Chapter 8

Just a Word

The call had started with nothing important.

Just the usual teasing.

"You know what?" Grace laughed. "You deserve an award."

"Oh?"

"For tolerating me."

Noel chuckled. "I deserve much more than an award."

She laughed even harder.

"Hahaha... but you're my..."

He froze.

"...princess."

Silence.

Grace's smile slowly grew.

"My?"

Noel shut his eyes for a second.

"You heard it wrong."

"I did?"

"I just said... you're a princess."

"Hmm..."

She let it go.

Or at least, she pretended to.

Neither of them brought it up again.

But they both knew exactly what had slipped out.

The conversation wandered from one random topic to another, as it always did.

After a while, Noel asked quietly,

"Can I ask you something?"

"Hmm?"

"What kind of boy do you actually like?"

Grace laughed.

"Interviewing me?"

"Maybe."

She smiled.

"Okay then..."

She took a deep breath.

"I want someone I'm completely comfortable with. The kind of person I can tell absolutely anything to without worrying he'll judge me. I don't need expensive gifts. I think I'd be happier if he randomly brought me a ten-rupee rose just because he wanted to see me smile."

Noel smiled to himself.

"I want someone who'll come and meet me whenever I say, 'I want to see you,' without asking a hundred questions first. I want someone who'll happily sit and listen to all my endless yapping instead of secretly wishing I'd stop talking. You know I talk a lot."

"I've noticed," Noel laughed.

She laughed with him.

"And I want someone who'll love me exactly as I am. I know I have flaws. I know I'm emotional. I overthink. I get stubborn. I cry over the smallest things. But I don't want to become someone else just to be loved."

She paused before continuing.

"At the same time... I want to become a better version of myself. I want him to look at me one day and feel proud to walk beside me. Not because he changed me... but because we helped each other grow."

Noel listened without saying a word.

"I have a career to build. So does he. I don't want either of us to sacrifice our dreams. I want us to build them together. I want someone I can proudly walk up to my mom and say... 'This is him.'"

There was another pause.

"And more than anything... I want someone who loves God. My spiritual journey isn't something that will ever end. I'll keep growing with God until the day I take my last breath. I don't expect him to have everything figured out. I don't either. But I hope we're walking in the same direction. I want someone who'll pray with me. Pray for me. Remind me of God's promises on the days I forget them. And let me do the same for him. I want us to help each other become more like Christ."

She smiled softly.

"I think that's beautiful."

Silence settled between them.

Then she laughed.

"And... I want him to be my best friend first. The rest can come later."

Noel looked out of his window.

The night breeze drifted into his room.

"So..." Grace asked.

"What?"

"What are you thinking?"

He smiled.

"I'm thinking..."

"Hmm?"

"That guy... whoever he is..."

"He'll be really lucky."

Grace laughed.

"I hope so."

They continued talking about random things for another hour, as though nothing important had been said.

But long after the call ended, one conversation stayed with Noel.

Every little thing Grace had wished for...

Without ever realizing it...

He had already been trying to become that person.

Not because she had described him.

But because caring for her had always come naturally.

And somewhere, without either of them knowing...

Friendship had begun quietly laying the foundation for something much deeper.



"Sometimes love doesn't begin with a confession. Sometimes it begins with becoming the answer to someone's prayer before either heart realizes it." 🤍
