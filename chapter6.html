Chapter 6

A Little Too Early

"Sometimes love is not spoken. It is delivered."

By 11 a.m., Grace had already argued with the printer, snapped at her colleague for borrowing a pen, and nearly cried because someone finished the last cup of coffee. "Grace, are you okay?" her colleague asked cautiously. "I'm fine." She wasn't. Everything felt irritating. Even silence felt loud. When Noel texted, "Had lunch?" She replied, "Can you stop asking me the same questions every single day?" She regretted it immediately. But not enough to fix it. On the other side of the city, Noel read it quietly. He didn’t reply. He just waited. Later, Grace called him crying. "My day was horrible..." He listened. Then he got up, took his bike keys, and left. He bought chocolates, chips, juices, and everything she liked. He packed them in a bag and left it outside her door. While still on the call, he said, "Open your door." She did. The bag was there. And so was he, walking away in the rain. She ran after him. "I'm sorry..." "It's okay," he said. "I love you so much... you're my best friend." He smiled. Because that was enough for him. She went back inside. Later, he texted her a banana emoji 🍌 "The universal solution." "Pazham pori." She laughed. A simple joke. A shared language. A deal was made that night. No explanations. Just 🍌. And somewhere between chaos and comfort… They started becoming home for each other.
